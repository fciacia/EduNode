MATHEMATICS — FRACTIONS

What a Fraction Is
A fraction represents parts of a whole. We write a fraction as a over b. The
bottom number, the denominator, tells how many equal parts the whole has been
divided into. The top number, the numerator, tells how many of those parts are
included. If a pizza is cut into four equal slices and you eat one slice, you have
eaten one quarter of the pizza, written 1/4.

Proper and Improper Fractions
When the numerator is smaller than the denominator, the fraction is a proper
fraction and its value is less than one, such as 1/2, 3/7, or 11/18. When the
numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator, the fraction is an improper
fraction and its value is one or more, such as 5/4, 3/2, or 7/3.

Mixed Numbers
An improper fraction can be written as a mixed number, which is a whole number
together with a proper fraction. For example, 8/5 is the same as 1 and 3/5. Mixed
numbers make a quantity easier to picture.

Equivalent Fractions
Different fractions can stand for the same amount. If you multiply the numerator
and the denominator by the same number, you get an equivalent fraction. So 1/2
equals 2/4, which also equals 4/8 — they all show the same part of a whole.
